Stockholm's Best Secondhand

Whether you're looking to score something fabulous for a bargain price, contribute to a good cause, or reduce your carbon footprint, you'll find some of the best thrift stores anywhere in Stockholm.

Myrorna

Kolargatan 2, 115 42 Stockholm

Located in Stockholm's Hjorthagen neighborhood, this is one of the biggest Myrornas in Sweden. With a large selection of clothing, housewares, furniture, books, toys, and more, it is also one of the nicest Myrornas with much of its merchandise donated by residents of Stockholm's posh Östermalm neighborhood nearby. Myrorna's profits go to the Salvation Army.

Emmaus

Peter Myndes Backe 8, 118 46 Stockholm

Conveniently located close to the Slussen metro stop in Stockholm's trendy Södermalm district, Emmaus has a great selection of clothing, housewares, books, and toys, as well as an entire vintage shop located in the basement. Profits from Emmaus go to several aid organizations in Africa and the Middle East.

Stadsmissionen Second Hand

Hornsgatan 58, 118 21 Stockholm

Across from Mariatorget in Stockholm's Södermalm district, Stadsmissionen is packed with a great selection of clothing, furniture, books, housewares, and more. Stadsmissionen's profits go towards helping the homeless, as well as elderly people and children in need in Sweden.

Röda Korset

Hornsgatan 54, 118 21 Stockholm

Also across from Mariatorget in Södermalm and just a few doors down from Stadsmissionen, Röda Korset is a smaller store but has a nice selection of clothing for adults and children and profits go to the humanitarian organization the Red Cross (Röda Korset in Swedish).

Judits Second Hand

Hornsgatan 75, 118 49 Stockholm

Down the street from Röda Korset and Stadsmissionen in the Södermalm district is Judits Second Hand. Selling clothing on consignment, Judits is smaller than some of the other thrift stores but has a carefully chosen selection, though profits do not go to a cause.

Filippa K Second Hand

Hornsgatan 75, 118 49 Stockholm

Next door to Judits (above) and under the same ownership, Filippa K Second Hand sells clothing and accessories only from minimalist Swedish designer Filippa K. With a special agreement with the designer, the store also sells samples and surplus from earlier collections, in addition to previously worn items. Like Judits, the store does not send profits to charity.

Beyond Retro Zinken

Brännkyrkagatan 82, 118 23 Stockholm

Based in the UK, Beyond Retro also has two locations in Stockholm, this one close to the Zinkensdamm metro stop in the Södermalm district, and another one closer to the city center on Drottninggatan. With a wide selection of clothing from the 60s, 70s, 80s, and 90s, including many name-brand items, Beyond Retro has men's, women's, and children's clothing and accessories.

Lisa Larsson Second Hand

Bondegatan 48, 116 33 Stockholm

Also boasting a large selection of quality vintage clothing and also located in the Södermalm district is Lisa Larsson Second Hand. Selling carefully chosen items on consignment, Lisa Larsson sells quality pieces but prices are a bit higher and profits do not go to charity.

Zak

Upplandsgatan 19A, 113 60 Stockholm

Located in Stockholm's fashionable Vasastan neighborhood, Zak is smaller but has a fine selection of mostly clothing and accessories for men and women. Prices are a bit higher, relative to Secondhand prices, but the selection is well curated and all merchandise is in relatively good condition.

Myrorna

Adolf Fredriks Kyrkogatan 5, 111 37 Stockholm

Though not as large as the Myrorna on Kolargatan (above), this Myrorna in the Vasastan neighborhood also boasts a nice selection of clothing, accessories, and housewares, though there are no children's clothes. Like all Myrornas, the store send its profits to the Salvation Army.
